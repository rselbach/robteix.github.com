Off-the-something Drama

Oct 22nd, 2012

Right in the very first issue of The Magazine, I found an interesting article called Baseball Misfits by Jason Snell. In it, Jason talks about the great divide that seems to exist between the geeks who love sports and those who despise them.

I am no stranger to that. I am one of the geeks who dig sports, I know both kinds and, exactly as mentioned in Jason’s article, I have experienced the wrath from the geeks who don’t dig sports when one who does happens to mention the subject.

Snell talked about why he loves sports so I thought I’d do the same. To me, sports are just another form of entertainment, like watching a movie or a TV show. One thing sports give me that TV shows usually don’t is the thrill of not knowing what is going to happen. But there’s also the same cheap thrills as in TV shows with overarching stories: the off-the-court/ice/field drama. I tend to follow sports in which the off-the-something drama is strongest. I love great rivalries, I love to know player X has this or that story that can have closure in tonight’s game. It’s all fun to me.

I think that’s one of the reasons I never fell in love with Soccer. I never felt that the off-the-field drama was explored enough. It may be a cultural thing. Who knows?
