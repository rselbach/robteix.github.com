Following Me on Social Media

Aug 19th, 2012

If you’re following me on some social media site (e.g Twitter, Google+, Facebook…), or if you’re thinking about following me, there are some things you should consider first.

I tend to overpost in batches. Sometimes I go days without posting anything and then, suddenly I feel all share-y and keep rambling about something for a while. I’ve got complaints before and so I thought you should be aware.

English is not my native language. Obviously. I will sound weird from most of the time. If you see something blatantly wrong and want to help, let me know so I learn. Mockery, however, is not very helpful but I’m a big boy and can take it. Using faulty English grammar as a way to distort an argument, however, will automatically make you lose said argument.

I do post in other languages from time to time. I mostly post in English but I do also post in Portuguese and French from time to time, mostly about things that are specific to certain places anyway and would not be of much interest for others. You should just try and ignore them as they’re really not that frequent, but if that bothers you too much, complaining about it won’t help. Best to unfollow. I will miss you, but that’s a price I’m willing to pay to continue interacting with my friends.

I post about things that interest me. Yes, it can sound selfish but that’s how I roll. Posting for others is something I have tried in the past and it was not pleasant. I post for myself. Sorry. Again, it should not be difficult to simply ignore posts about some topics you’re not interested in, but if you can’t, just unfollow. I’ll be sad but c’est la vie.

I am an atheist. I know that in 2012 (when I’m writing this) it should not matter, but unfortunately it still does in parts of the world. I’ve mostly stopped posting about atheism, so this should definitely not bother you too much, but I know it can be important for some people–although I honestly can’t understand it as I follow plenty of theists.

I don’t always follow back. Not because I’m trying to be an ungrateful dick, but it’s just that if everybody follows everybody back, then what’s the point? I’ve heard self-appointed social media gurus claiming to have no issues following tens of thousands of people. Good for them but I sure as heck can’t keep up. Don’t take it personally, most people I follow don’t follow me back either.

That’s it I suppose. If you’re cool with the above and I really hope you are, follow me now!
